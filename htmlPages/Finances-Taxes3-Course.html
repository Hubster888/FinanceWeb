Taxes

3) Conclusion

So now you’ve made it to the end of the taxes section I feel like you need a little extra motivation to get to work with all the tax saving tips.
Let me tell you a story about Bob.
Bob’s your average guy with an average education, your average job and still living pay check to pay check. But Bob has gained a super power, he got to work on his tax saving.
Now Bob can get an extra income of around £1000 every year with just a little effort. You want to know how? Let's learn a little about Bob.
Bob is a teacher earning £25,000 at the young age of 30. Since he is single with a baby boy, he is eligible for working tax credit of £2,045 per year. That there is already a huge increase for him while he has also found out he can apply for a council tax reduction giving him even more disposable income.
But Bob is not stupid, he doesn't spend all his money, instead he puts some of it into a pensions scheme while another little bit into his ISA.
Now Bob has put a decent amount into his retirement and got an extra £1,000 per year.
Bob’s even smarter than we thought and he had invested in apple many years ago. Now he’s looking at grabbing an extra profit of £20,000, but oh no, with the CGT he will lose £4,000.
Using the knowledge Bob has gained he transferred half his stocks to a trusted friend. Then Bob cash in the stocks and Bob pays no tax! As the split stocks are worth £10,000 each which is under the personal allowance.
I’ve not only made up but have left out a lot of details out about Bob so his story may seem exaggerated. But the truth is that taking effort to research and take action on your taxes can make a huge difference to your life.
I strongly encourage you to check out some of the other articles related to saving but do extra research outside of here to get more details but remember to take action.
